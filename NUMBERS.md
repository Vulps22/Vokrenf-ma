# Numbers in Vokrenfōma

Vokrenfōma uses a base-10 system. Digits 0–9 and scale words are dūn; larger values are formed by compounding them into dūnma following the same left-to-right logic as all other compound words.

## Constructing Numbers

Multi-digit numbers are dūnma formed by combining digit words in order from most significant to least significant.

* **10** = un + nūl → **unūl** (one-zero)
* **34** = sol + lit → **sollit** (three-four)
* **78** = nil + pōl → **nilpōl** (seven-eight)

Scale words (s̄an, tor, nar) compound with a digit to express multiples of that scale:

* **s̄anrif** = s̄an × 2 = 200 (hundred-two)
* **tormōt** = tor × 5 = 5,000 (thousand-five)
* **tortor** = tor × tor = 1,000,000 (thousand-thousand)
* **nartor** = nar × tor = 10¹² (billion-thousand)

## Cardinal vs Ordinal

Position relative to the noun determines whether a number expresses quantity or order.

**Cardinal (quantity):** Number placed *after* the noun.

* namlok rif = "two kitchens"
* pah unnūl = "ten years" (a quantity of ten individual years)

**Ordinal (sequence):** Number placed *before* the noun.

* sol nōksolnūl = "the third month" (a specific position in a sequence)
* unnūl pah = "the tenth year"

## Named Spans

When a noun and number fuse into a single dūnma, they name a fixed span rather than express a count:

* **nilnōk** = nōk (day) + nil (seven) → "week"
* **pahunnūl** = pah (year) + unnūl (ten) → "decade"

Named spans behave as singular nouns and can themselves be pluralised with ma or counted with a number.
