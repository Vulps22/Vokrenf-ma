# Lettering System

Vokrenfōma uses a Latin-based writing system extended with diacritical marks. Text is written left-to-right.

## Diacritical Marks

Vokrenfōma uses two diacritical marks to create variant letters:

**Macron (¯)** — placed above a letter to mark a modified form:
* **ū** — long form of u; /uː/ vs. u /ʌ/
* **ō** — long form of o; /oʊ/ vs. o /ɒ/
* **ī** — distinct short vowel; /ɪ/ vs. i /iː/ (note: for ī, the macron marks a *shorter* sound, not longer)
* **c̄** — marks the velar fricative /x/, a sound not represented by any plain Latin letter
* **s̄** — marks the voiceless /s/, distinguishing it from plain s /z/

**Acute accent (´)** — marks the vowel **á** /ja/ ("ya"), a core vowel present in the language since its earliest words (e.g. **ná** /nja/). Distinct from plain **a** /a/.

## Notes on Specific Letters

**s and s̄** are two distinct phonemes despite their visual similarity. Plain **s** is voiced /z/; marked **s̄** is voiceless /s/. They are treated as separate letters.

**c** does not exist as a standalone letter. Only the marked form **c̄** /x/ is in use.

**ar** — the combination of **a** followed by **r** is r-colored toward /ɑr/, as in *nar* /nɑr/. This is the only context where **a** deviates from its default /a/ value.
